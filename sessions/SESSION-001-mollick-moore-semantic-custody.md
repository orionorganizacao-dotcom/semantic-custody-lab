# SESSION-001 — Mollick, Moore's Law, and Semantic Custody

## Status

Exploratory session.

This document records the genealogy of ideas that led from a discussion of Moore's Law and AI-assisted academic revision to the concept of semantic custody.

The purpose of this document is historical traceability, not protocol specification.

---

## Entry Point

Trigger:

Ethan Mollick's experiment using GPT-5.5 to revisit and extend an earlier academic paper on Moore's Law.

Core question:

What happens when AI can continuously revise knowledge objects that were previously considered finished?

---

## Discovery Sequence

1. Metric Capture by Success

4. OA-001
5. OA-002

9. Dissolution of authority into navigability
10. Temporal addressing of semantic states

---

## Key Distinction

Custody ≠ Governance

Custody concerns traceability.

Governance concerns authority.

The session progressively replaced authority-centric formulations with reconstruction-centric formulations.

---

## Open Outcome

HYP-001 — Temporal Addressing of Semantic Objects

Objects of knowledge may not require central authority to manage semantic change.

They may require sufficient temporal addressing to keep competing semantic states navigable and reconstructible.
